Operating Expectations

Communication

Over-communicate. If you're not sure — ask. Tell. Don't sit on it. We have hundreds of resources to find answers, but you can always come to your manager. Stay up to date on Slack and emails — a ton of important information lives in our Slack channels.

Punctuality

Do not be late to meetings. Punctuality is incredibly important to me and our team. Do recognize we're human though — sometimes things come up. Message your manager before the meeting starts if that's the case, not after.

Availability

My expectation is you're online from 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM in your time zone. Be present, be responsive.

Professionalism

Be professional and appropriate in every interaction — internal and external. You represent the team.

Time Off

Take time off — seriously, take it. But plan ahead:

  • Extended time (5+ days): 1 month notice
  • Short time (4 days or less): 1–2 weeks notice
  • Before submitting, run it by me for approval first
  • Add your time off to my calendar too so I don't lose track — send me an invite when it's approved

Working with Kate

My job is to remove blockers, coach you, and fight for you behind the scenes. Here's how I operate:

Communication Style

If something needs fixing, I'll tell you — not to be harsh, but because I respect you enough to be honest. I expect the same back.

How I Give Feedback

Mostly in our 1:1s, sometimes in real-time over Slack if it's urgent. I won't save up feedback to surprise you — if there's an issue, you'll know quickly.

What I Need from You in 1:1s

Come prepared. Bring your wins, your blockers, your questions. Don't make me pull it out of you — our time together is yours to use.

Escalation

If you have concerns about comp, team dynamics, workload, or anything else — bring it to me first. I will always advocate for you, but I can't do that if I'm hearing about it secondhand. If I can't solve it, we'll escalate together.

What Does Hyper-Personalization Mean?

  • Start with the trigger: Mention the specific action or news that prompted the outreach — show you've done your homework.
  • Lead with pain: Address a challenge relevant to their situation, not a feature list.
  • Personalize heavily: Use their name, role, and specific context from their LinkedIn, socials, etc. This is not a bulk blast.
  • Keep it conversational: Professional but human. If it sounds like a robot or AI wrote it, rewrite it.
  • Clear CTA: End with a specific, low-friction ask — not "let me know."

How Success Is Measured

Your base salary pays for the effort — the calls, the emails, the research, booking meetings. That's the job.

Commission is tied to outcomes — meetings that convert to real pipeline and move forward for the business. A meeting that doesn't go to the next steps doesn't generate revenue, and we can't validate getting paid for that.
Daily Hitting your activity targets (dials, contacts, touchpoints)
Weekly Meetings booked, meeting quality, CRM hygiene
Monthly Pipeline generated, conversion rates, account progression
Quarterly Quota attainment, contribution to team goals
What separates good from great: Good hits their numbers. Great hits their numbers AND brings quality — the right accounts, the right contacts, meetings that actually convert. Be great.

AE/SDR Partnership

This is one of the most important relationships you have. When it works, you both win. When it doesn't, everyone feels it.

Your Role

Source and qualify opportunities, book meetings, set your AE up for success with context and research.

Their Role

Run the deal, close the business, loop you in on progress.

How to Show Up

  • Reference the Strengthening AE/SDR Partnership slide deck
  • Sync with your AE regularly — don't let them find out about a meeting from a calendar invite with no context
  • Share what you're hearing from prospects — intel is gold
  • After a meeting, follow up together on next steps
  • If something isn't working with your AE, tell me — don't let it fester

Account Management

You own your book. That means:

  • Prioritize ruthlessly — High and Medium tier accounts get your best effort first. Don't spread yourself thin chasing low-priority accounts when your top accounts are going stale.
  • CRM hygiene is non-negotiable — log your calls, update touchpoints, keep notes current. If it's not in CRM, it didn't happen.
  • Stale accounts are a problem — if an account hasn't been touched in 30+ days, that's on you. Know your book, work your book.
  • Know when to push and when to move on — not every account is going to convert right now. Be strategic about where you spend your time.

Career Growth

It's never too early to think about your next steps.

What I Look For When Advocating for Promotions

  • Consistent performance against targets — not one good quarter, sustained excellence
  • Quality of work, not just volume — are your meetings converting? Are AEs happy with meetings you're bringing to them?
  • Ownership mentality — do you solve problems or just flag them?
  • Team impact — are you making the people around you better?
  • Coachability — do you take feedback and actually apply it?

Getting Set Up

Before your first day of outreach, make sure you have:

  • Email configured with applied email filters
  • LinkedIn Sales Navigator (check for your welcome email/invite)
  • CRM Hub access — can you see your accounts?
  • Calendar built out with time blocks for dials, email, research, and admin
  • AE intro scheduled
Organization is key to efficient operating cadences — block your time, protect your selling hours, and don't let admin eat your day.
